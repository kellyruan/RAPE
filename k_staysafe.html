How to Help a Survivor

  • Believe in them.

    Being able to even share the experience takes a great deal of strength and courage. Let them know that you believe them. NEVER blame the survivor, it was not their fault.

  • Be respectful of privacy and confidentiality.

    Do not tell others about the assault without the survivors permission.

  • Provide information.

    Inform them about the possble resources that are available such as the hotline, counseling, medical assistance, preserving evidence, or reporting to the police.

  • Be a good listener

    Recovering takes a ling time. Let the survivor choose what information they want to share. Remember to concentrate on their feelings, allow silences,let them know that you are glad that they disclosed to you, don't interrogate them, and don't ask tell them what they "should" have done.

    Ask the survivor what they may need assiantance with. Do not do anything based on your feelings, such as beating up the assailant.

  • Know your limitations

    No one has superpowers. Provide as much as you can and let the survivor know that it's okay to talk to others if they need more help.

What to do after the Assault- for Survivors

  • Remember that the incident was not your fault.

    Something happened to you that you didn’t want to happen—and that’s not OK.

  • Know that you are not the only one.

    Find support, whether it be in your friends, other survivors, mediccal assistance, or in counseling.

  • Have evidence.

    This is an important step if you want to report your experience to the police or bring it to a court of law.

Staying Safe in Certain Situations

Staying Safe in Social Situations:

1. Be aware of your surroundings at all times.

2. Drink responsibly.

3. Don't leave your drinks unattended.

4. Stick with your friends.

5. Stay safe at clubs and bars.

6. Be assertive.

7. Keep personal information private.

8. Always keep your phone charged.




Staying Safe When You're Alone:

1. Be aware of your surroundings at all times.

2. Be careful about using technology when you're out alone.

3. Trust your natural instincts.

4. Carry defensive items if you know how to safely use them.

5. Walk to your destination with confidence.

6. Make eye contact if you are being followed by someone who may be a potential threat.

7. Keep in mind that rapists do not necessarily look any different than normal people.

8. Don't underestimate your abilities and improvisation skills.

What to do if You're Attacked:

1. Utilize self defense and "SING" (Solar Plexus-Instep-Nose-Groin), the 4 points of attack.

2. Yell, shout, and draw attention to yourself.

3. Notice and leave identifying marks on the attacker.

4. Remember that you have full rights to defend yourself in every way.
